Now back to the original problem. We will be making 60 monthly payments of $420. The present value of all 60 payments is 6 

\begin{align*} \amp 420(1+r)^{-1}+420(1+r)^{-2}+\cdots +420(1+r)^{-60} \\ \amp \hskip1in =420\frac{(1+r)^{-1}-(1+r)^{-61} }{1-(1+r)^{-1} } \\ \amp \hskip1in=420\frac{1-(1+r)^{-60} }{(1+r)-1}\\ \amp \hskip1in =420\frac{1-(1+r)^{-60} }{r} \end{align*}

The interest rate \(100r\%\) being charged by the car dealer is such that the present value of 60 monthly payments of $420 is $23520. That is, the monthly interest rate being charged by the car dealer is the solution of

\begin{align*} 23520=420\frac{1-(1+r)^{-60} }{r} \qquad\amp \text{or}\qquad 56=\frac{1-(1+r)^{-60} }{r} \\ \amp \text{or}\qquad 56r=1-(1+r)^{-60} \\ \amp \text{or}\qquad 56r(1+r)^{60}=(1+r)^{60}-1 \\ \amp \text{or}\qquad (1-56r)(1+r)^{60}=1 \end{align*}

Set \(f(r)=(1-56r)(1+r)^{60}-1\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} f'(r)=-56(1+r)^{60}+60(1-56r)(1+r)^{59} \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} f'(r)=\big[-56(1+r)+60(1-56r)\big](1+r)^{59} =(4-3416r)(1+r)^{59} \end{equation*}

Apply Newton's method with an initial guess of \(r_1=.002\text{.}\) (That's \(0.2\)% per month or 2.4% per year.) Then

\begin{align*} r_2\amp =r_1-\frac{(1-56r_1)(1+r_1)^{60}-1}{(4-3416r_1)(1+r_1)^{59}}=0.002344\\ \ r_3\amp =r_2-\frac{(1-56r_2)(1+r_2)^{60}-1}{(4-3416r_2)(1+r_2)^{59}}=0.002292\\ r_4\amp =r_3-\frac{(1-56r_3)(1+r_3)^{60}-1}{(4-3416r_3)(1+r_3)^{59}}=0.002290\\ r_5\amp =r_4-\frac{(1-56r_4)(1+r_4)^{60}-1}{(4-3416r_4)(1+r_4)^{59}}=0.002290 \end{align*}

So the interest rate is 0.229% per month or 2.75% per year.
