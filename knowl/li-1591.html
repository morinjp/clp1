Asymptotes: In the problem statement, we are told:

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to \pm \infty}\frac{1+2x}{e^{x^2}}=0 \end{equation*}

So, \(y=0\) is a horizontal asymptote both at \(x=\infty\) and at \(x=-\infty\text{.}\)

Since \(f(x)\) is continuous, it has no vertical asymptotes.
