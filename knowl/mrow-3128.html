\begin{align*} f(x)&=x^3\left[2\log x - \frac{11}{3}\right]\\ f'(x)&=x^3\left[\frac{2}{x}\right]+3x^2\left[2\log x - \frac{11}{3}\right]=6x^2\log x -9x^2\\ f''(x)&=6x^2\cdot\frac{1}{x}+12x\log x - 18x=12x\log x - 12 x\\ f'''(x)&=12x\cdot\frac{1}{x}+12\log x - 12 = 12\log x\\ \color{blue}{f'''(a)}&\color{blue}{=12\log a}\\ \end{align*}

Now, let's move to the Taylor polynomial. Remember that \(e\) is a constant.

\begin{align*} T_3(x)&=-\frac{2}{3}\sqrt{e^3}+3ex-6\sqrt{e}x^2+x^3\\ T_3'(x)&=3e-12\sqrt{e}x+3x^2\\ T_3''(x)&=-12\sqrt{e}+6x\\ T_3'''(x)&=6\\ \color{red}{T_3'''(a)}&\color{red}{=6}\\ \end{align*}

The final bullet point gives us the equation:

\begin{align*} \color{blue}{f'''(a)}&=\color{red}{T_3'''(a)}\\ \color{blue}{12\log a} &= \color{red}{6}\\ \log a &= \frac{1}{2}\\ a&=e^{\tfrac{1}{2}} \end{align*}
