Exercise Group 3.6.7.5–9

Exercises — Stage 2

In Questions 3.6.7.7 through 3.6.7.10, find the symmetries of a function from its equation.

5
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\frac{x^4-x^6}{e^{x^2}} \end{equation*}

Show that \(f(x)\) is even.

6
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\sin(x)+\cos\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \end{equation*}

Show that \(f(x)\) is periodic.

7
\begin{equation*} f(x)=x^4+5x^2+\cos\left(x^3\right) \end{equation*}

What symmetries (even, odd, periodic) does \(f(x)\) have?

8
\begin{equation*} f(x)=x^5+5x^4 \end{equation*}

What symmetries (even, odd, periodic) does \(f(x)\) have?

9
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\tan\left(\pi x\right) \end{equation*}

What is the period of \(f(x)\text{?}\)
