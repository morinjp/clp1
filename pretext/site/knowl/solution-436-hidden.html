• If we write \(z(t)\) for the length of the ladder at time \(t\) and \(y(t)\) for the height of the top end of the ladder at time \(t\) we have

    \begin{equation*} z(t)^2 = 5^2 + y(t)^2. \end{equation*}
  • We differentiate the above equation with respect to \(t\) and get
    \begin{align*} 2z \cdot z' &= 2y \cdot y', \end{align*}
  • We are told that \(z'(t)=-2\text{,}\) so \(z(3.5)=20-3.5\cdot2 = 13\text{.}\)
  • At this point \(y = \sqrt{z^2-5^2} = \sqrt{169-25} = \sqrt{144} = 12\text{.}\)
  • Hence
    \begin{align*} 2\cdot 13 \cdot (-2) &= 2 \cdot 12 y'\\ y' &= -\frac{2\cdot 13}{12} = -\frac{13}{6} \text{ meters per second}. \end{align*}
