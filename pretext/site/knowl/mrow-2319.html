\begin{align*} A&=\frac{\theta}{2\pi}(35\pi)=\frac{35}{2}\theta\\ \end{align*}

This is the formula we wanted, relating \(A\) and \(\theta\text{.}\) Differentiating with respect to \(t\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \diff{A}{t}&=\frac{35}{2}\diff{\theta}{t}=\frac{35}{2}\left(-\frac{\pi}{6}\right)=-\frac{35\pi}{12}\\ \end{align*}

Since \(\ds\diff{S}{t}=\frac{1}{5}\ds\diff{A}{t}\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \diff{S}{t}&=-\frac{1}{5}\frac{35\pi}{12}=-\frac{7\pi}{12}\approx -1.8\;\frac{\mathrm{cm}^3}{\mathrm{sec}^2} \end{align*}
