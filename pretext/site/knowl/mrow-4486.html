\begin{align*} \diff{}{x}\left\{-\cos\left(x^2\right)\right\}&=\sin\left(x^2\right)\cdot2x\\ \end{align*}

This differs from \(f(x)\) only by a constant multiple.

\begin{align*} \diff{}{x}\left\{-\frac{5}{2}\cos\left(x^2\right)\right\}&=\frac{5}{2}\sin\left(x^2\right)\cdot2x=5x\sin(x^2)=f(x) \end{align*}
