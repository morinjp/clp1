First, let's consider the behaviour of exponential functions \(a^x\) based on whether \(a\) is greater or less than 1. As we know, \(\ds\lim_{x\to\infty}a^x =\left\{\begin{array}{ll} \infty & a \gt 1\\ 0&a \lt 1 \end{array}\right.\) and \(\ds\lim_{x\to-\infty}a^x=\left\{\begin{array}{ll} 0 & a \gt 1\\ \infty&a \lt 1 \end{array}\right.\text{.}\) Our function has \(\ds\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)=\infty\) and \(\ds\lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x)=0\text{,}\) so we conclude \(a \gt 1\text{:}\) thus \((d)\) and also \((b)\) hold. (We could have also seen that \((b)\) holds because \(a^x\) is defined for all real numbers.)
