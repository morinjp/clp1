Since \(T_3(x)\) is the third-degree Taylor polynomial for \(f(x)\) about \(x=1\text{:}\)

  • \(T_3(1)=f(1)\)
  • \(T_3'(1)=f'(1)\)
  • \(T_3''(1)=f''(1)\)
  • \(T_3'''(1)=f'''(1)\)

In particular, \(f''(1)=T_3''(1)\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} T_3'(x)&=3x^2-10x+9\\ T_3''(x)&=6x-10\\ T_3''(1)&=6-10=-4 \end{align*}

So, \(f''(1)=-4\text{.}\)
