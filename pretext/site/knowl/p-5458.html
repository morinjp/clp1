Now we repeat, but starting with the (second) guess \(x_2\) rather than \(x_1\text{.}\) This gives the (third) guess \(x_3= x_2 -\frac{f(x_2)}{f'(x_2)}\text{.}\) And so on. By way of summary, Newton's method is

  1. Make a preliminary guess \(x_1\text{.}\)
  2. Define \(x_2=x_1-\frac{f(x_1)}{f'(x_1)}\text{.}\)

  3. Iterate. That is, for each natural number \(n\text{,}\) once you have computed \(x_n\text{,}\) define

    Equation C.1.1 (Newton's method)
    \begin{equation*} x_{n+1}=x_n-\frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)} \end{equation*}
