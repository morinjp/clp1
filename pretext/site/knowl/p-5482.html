Let \(f(x)\) be a continuous function. The secant method is a variant of Newton's method that avoids the use of the derivative of \(f(x)\) â€” which can be very helpful when dealing with the derivative is not easy. It avoids the use of the derivative by approximating \(f'(x)\) by \(\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\) for some \(h\text{.}\) That is, it approximates the tangent line to \(f\) at \(x\) by a secant line for \(f\) that passes through \(x\text{.}\) To limit the number of evaluations of \(f(x)\) required, it uses \(x=x_{n-1}\) and \(x+h=x_n\text{.}\) Here is how it works.
