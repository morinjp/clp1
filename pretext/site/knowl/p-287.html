Note — be careful to include the superscript \(+\) and \(-\) when writing these limits. You might also see the following notations:

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) &= \lim_{x \to a+} f(x) = \lim_{x \downarrow a} f(x) = \lim_{x \searrow a} f(x) = L & \text{right-hand limit}\\ \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) &= \lim_{x \to a-} f(x) = \lim_{x \uparrow a} f(x) = \lim_{x \nearrow a} f(x) = L & \text{left-hand limit} \end{align*}

but please use with the notation in Definition 1.3.7 above.
