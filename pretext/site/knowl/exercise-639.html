A function \(f(x)\) defined on the whole real number line satisfies the following conditions

\begin{equation*} f(0)=0\qquad f(2)=2\qquad \lim_{x\rightarrow+\infty}f(x)=0\qquad f'(x)=K(2x-x^2)e^{-x} \end{equation*}

for some positive constant \(K\text{.}\) (Read carefully: you are given the derivative of \(f(x)\text{,}\) not \(f(x)\) itself.)

  1. Determine the intervals on which \(f\) is increasing and decreasing and the location of any local maximum and minimum values of \(f\text{.}\)
  2. Determine the intervals on which \(f\) is concave up or down and the \(x\)--coordinates of any inflection points of \(f\text{.}\)
  3. Determine \(\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow-\infty}f(x)\text{.}\)
  4. Sketch the graph of \(y=f(x)\text{,}\) showing any asymptotes and the information determined in parts 3.6.7.11.a and 3.6.7.11.b.
