Solution 3.4.11.9.1

We use the function \(f(x)=\sin(x)\) and point \(a=\pi\) as the centre of our approximation since we know that

\begin{align*} \sin(a)&=f(\pi)=\sin{\pi}=0. \end{align*}

and \(\pi\) is reasonably close to \(\dfrac{101\pi}{100}\text{.}\) We compute \(f'(x)=\cos(x)\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} f'(\pi)&=\cos(\pi) = -1.\\ \end{align*}

So, the linear approximation of \(\sin\left(\frac{101\pi}{100}\right) \) is

\begin{align*} f\left(\frac{101\pi}{100}\right)&\approx T_1\left(\frac{101\pi}{100}\right)\\ &= f(\pi) + f'(\pi)\cdot \left(\frac{101\pi}{100}-\pi\right)\\ &=0+(-1)\cdot\frac{\pi}{100}=-\frac{\pi}{100}. \end{align*}
