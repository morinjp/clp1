    \begin{equation*} \left|\frac{f'''(c)}{3!}\cdot (2-1)^3\right| = \left|\frac{c}{6(22-c^2)}\right| \end{equation*}

  • When \(1\leq c\leq2\text{,}\) we know that \(18 \leq 22-c^2 \leq 21\text{,}\) and that numerator and denominator are non-negative, so
    \begin{align*} \left|\frac{c}{6(22-c^2)}\right| &=\frac{c}{6(22-c^2)} \leq \frac{2}{6(22-c^2)} \leq \frac{2}{6\cdot 18}\\ & = \frac{1}{54} \leq \frac{1}{50} \end{align*}
    as required.
  • Alternatively, notice that \(c\) is an increasing function of \(c\text{,}\) while \(22-c^2\) is a decreasing function of \(c\text{.}\) Hence the fraction is an increasing function of \(c\) and takes its largest value at \(c=2\text{.}\) Hence
    \begin{align*} \left|\frac{c}{6(22-c^2)}\right| & \leq \frac{2}{6\times 18} = \frac{1}{54} \leq \frac{1}{50}. \end{align*}
