Solution 2.12.2.25.1

This limit represents the derivative computed at \(x=1\) of the function \(f(x)=\arctan x\text{.}\) To see this, simply use the definition of the derivative at \(a=1\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \left.\diff{}{x}\{f(x)\}\right|_{a} &= \lim_{x \to a}\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}\\ \left.\diff{}{x}\{\arctan x\}\right|_{1} &=\lim_{x\to1} \frac{\arctan x-\arctan 1}{x-1}\\ &=\lim_{x\to1} \frac{\arctan x-\frac{\pi}{4}}{x-1}\\ &=\displaystyle \lim_{x\to 1}\left( (x-1)^{-1}\left(\arctan x - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)\right). \end{align*}

Since the derivative of \(f(x)\) is \(\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}\text{,}\) its value at \(x=1\) is exactly \(\dfrac{1}{2}\text{.}\)
