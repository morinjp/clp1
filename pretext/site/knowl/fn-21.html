Note that the coordinates \((x,y)\) is an ordered pair of two numbers \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\) Traditionally the first number is called the abscissa while the second is the ordinate, but these terms are a little archaic. It is now much more common to hear people refer to the first number as the \(x\)-coordinate and the second as \(y\)-coordinate.in-context
