The derivative of the function is

\begin{align*} \frac{(1-x^2)\cdot\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} - \sqrt{x} \cdot (-2x)}{(1-x^2)^2} &= \frac{(1-x^2) - 2x \cdot (-2x)}{2\sqrt{x}(1-x^2)^2} \end{align*}

The derivative is undefined if either \(x \lt 0\) or \(x = 0,\pm 1\) (since the square-root is undefined for \(x \lt 0\) and the denominator is zero when \(x=0,1,-1\text{.}\) Putting this together â€” the derivative exists for \(x \gt 0, x\neq 1\text{.}\)
