The function \(f_4(x)\) is the ratio
\begin{equation*} f_4(x)=\frac{1}{f_5(x)}\qquad\text{with}\qquad f_5(x)=3x+1 \end{equation*}
If we can find the derivative of \(f_5(x)\text{,}\) we will be able to get the derivative of \(f_4(x)\) just by applying the special case the quotient rule (CorollaryÂ 2.4.6). The derivative of \(f_5(x)\) is easy.
