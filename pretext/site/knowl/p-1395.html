In order to create the tangent line (as we have done a few times now) we squeeze \(h \to 0\text{.}\) As we do this, the secant through \(\big(a,f(a)\big)\) and \(\big(a+h,f(a+h)\big)\) approaches  3  the tangent line to \(y=f(x)\) at \(x=a\text{.}\) Since the secant becomes the tangent line in this limit, the slope of the secant becomes the slope of the tangent and

\begin{align*} \big[\text{the slope of the tangent line to $\;y=f(x)$ at $x=a$}\big] &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0}\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}\\ &=f'(a). \end{align*}
