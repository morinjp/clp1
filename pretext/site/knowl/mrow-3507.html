So, the linear approximation of \(\sin\left(\frac{101\pi}{100}\right) \) is

\begin{align*} f\left(\frac{101\pi}{100}\right)&\approx T_1\left(\frac{101\pi}{100}\right)\\ &= f(\pi) + f'(\pi)\cdot \left(\frac{101\pi}{100}-\pi\right)\\ &=0+(-1)\cdot\frac{\pi}{100}=-\frac{\pi}{100}. \end{align*}
