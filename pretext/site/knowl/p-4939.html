3.6.7.12.d Since \(g\) and \(f\) are inverses,

\begin{align*} g(f(x))&=x\\ \end{align*}

Using the chain rule,

\begin{align*} g'(f(x))\cdot f'(x)&=1\\ \end{align*}

Since \(f'(x)=-e^{-x}=-f(x)\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} g'(f(x))\cdot f(x)&=-1\\ \end{align*}

We plug in \(f(x)=\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} g'\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\cdot \frac{1}{2}&=-1\\ g'\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)&=-2 \end{align*}
