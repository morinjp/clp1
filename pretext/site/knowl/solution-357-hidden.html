Since \(g(y)=f^{-1}(y)\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} f(g(y))&=f\left(f^{-1}(y)\right)=y\\ \end{align*}

Now, we can differentiate with respect to \(y\) using the chain rule.

\begin{align*} \diff{}{y}\left\{f(g(y))\right\}&=\diff{}{y}\{y\}\\ f'(g(y))\cdot g'(y)&=1\\ g'(y)&=\frac{1}{f'(g(y))}=\frac{1}{1-\sin g(y)} \end{align*}
