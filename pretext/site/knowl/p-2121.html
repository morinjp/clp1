Just after CorollaryÂ 2.6.17, we said that we would, in the future, find the derivative of \(x^a\) for all real numbers. The future is here. Let \(x\gt 0\) and \(a\) be any real number. Exponentiating both sides of \(\log\big(x^a\big)=a\log x\) gives us \(x^a=e^{a\log x}\) and then

\begin{align*} \diff{}{x} x^a &= \diff{}{x} e^{a\log x} = e^{a\log x} \diff{}{x}(a\log x) &\text{by the chain rule} \\ &=\frac{a}{x} e^{a\log x} =\frac{a}{x} x^a \\ &=a x^{a-1} \end{align*}

as expected.
