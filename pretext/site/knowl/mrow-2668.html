\begin{align*} T(t)&=[100-A]e^{Kt}+A\\ \end{align*}

where \(A\) is the room temperature, and \(K\) is some constant. We are told that \(T(15)=85\) and \(T(30)=73\text{,}\) so:

\begin{align*} 85=T(15)&=[100-A]e^{15K}+A\\ 73=T(30)&=[100-A]e^{30K}+A\\ \end{align*}

Rearranging both equations:

\begin{align*} \frac{85-A}{100-A}&=e^{15K}\\ \frac{73-A}{100-A}&=e^{30K}=\left(e^{15K}\right)^2\\ \end{align*}

Using these equations:

\begin{align*} \left(\frac{85-A}{100-A}\right)^2=\left(e^{15K}\right)^2&=e^{30K}=\frac{73-A}{100-A}\\ \frac{(85-A)^2}{100-A}&=73-A\\ (85-A)^2&=(73-A)(100-A)\\ 85^2-170A+A^2&=7300-173A+A^2\\ 173A-170A&=7300-85^2\\ 3A&=75\\ A&=25 \end{align*}
