Footnote 1.6.8
The similar (but uglier) formula for solving cubics took until the 15th century and the work of del Ferro and Cardano (and Cardano's student Ferrari). A similar (but even uglier) formula for quartics was also found by Ferrari. The extremely famous Abel-Ruffini Theorem (nearly by Ruffini in the late 18th century and completely by Abel in early 19th century) demonstrates that a similar formula for the zeros of a quintic does not exist. Note that the theorem does not say that quintics do not have zeros; rather it says that the zeros cannot in general be expressed using a finite combination of addition, multiplication, division, powers and roots. The interested student should also look up Évariste Galois and his contributions to this area.in-context
