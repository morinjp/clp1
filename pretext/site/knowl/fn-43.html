Footnote 1.6.1
Remember that \(\sin\) and \(\cos\) are defined on all real numbers, so \(\tan(x) = \sin(x)/\cos(x)\) is continuous everywhere except where \(\cos(x)=0\text{.}\) This happens when \(x = \frac{\pi}{2}+n\pi\) for any integer \(n\text{.}\) If you cannot remember where \(\tan(x)\) “blows up” or \(\sin(x)=0\) or \(\cos(x)=0\) then you should definitely revise trigonometric functions. Come to think of it — just revise them anyway.in-context
