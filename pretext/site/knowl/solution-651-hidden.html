If we plug in \(x=0\) to the numerator and denominator, both are zero, so this is a candidate for l'HÃ´pital's Rule. However, an easier way to evaluate the limit is to factor \(x^2\) from the numerator and denominator, and cancel.

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sqrt{x^6+4x^4}}{x^2\cos x} &=\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sqrt{x^4}\sqrt{x^2+4}}{x^2\cos x}\\ &=\lim_{x\to0}\frac{x^2\sqrt{x^2+4}}{x^2\cos x}\\ &=\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sqrt{x^2+4}}{\cos x}\\ &=\frac{\sqrt{0^2+4}}{\cos(0)}=2 \end{align*}
