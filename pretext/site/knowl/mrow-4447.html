\begin{align*} f(x)&=\frac{3}{3}x^3-\frac{9}{2}x^2+4x+C\\ &=x^3-\frac{9}{2}x^2+4x+C\\ \end{align*}

Use the fact that \(f(1)=10\) to solve for \(C\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 10&=1-\frac{9}{2}+4+C\\ C&=\frac{19}{2}\\ \end{align*}

All together,

\begin{align*} f(x)&=x^3-\frac{9}{2}x^2+4x+\frac{19}{2}. \end{align*}
