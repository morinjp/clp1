If the derivative is positive, the function is increasing, so let's start by finding the derivative. We use the product rule (although QuestionÂ 2.7.3.12 gives a shortcut).

\begin{align*} f'(x)&=1\cdot e^x+xe^x=(1+x)e^x \end{align*}

Since \(e^x\) is always positive, \(f'(x) \gt 0\) when \(1+x \gt 0\text{.}\) So, \(f(x)\) is increasing when \(x \gt -1\text{.}\)
