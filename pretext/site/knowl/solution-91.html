Solution 1.5.2.13.1

We have, after dividing both numerator and denominator by \(x^2\) (which is the highest power of the denominator) that

\begin{equation*} \frac{5x^2-3x+1}{3x^2+x+7}=\frac{5-\frac{3}{x}+\frac{1}{x^2}}{3+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{7}{x^2}}. \end{equation*}

Since \(1/x\to 0\) and also \(1/x^2\to 0\) as \(x\to +\infty\text{,}\) we conclude that

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to +\infty} \frac{5x^2-3x+1}{3x^2 +x+7}=\frac{5}{3}. \end{equation*}
