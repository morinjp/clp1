We differentiate a few time to find the pattern.

\begin{align*} \diff{}{x}\{2^x\}&=2^x\log 2\\ \ddiff{2}{}{x}\{2^x\}&=2^x\log2 \cdot \log 2 = 2^x(\log2)^2\\ \ddiff{3}{}{x}\{2^x\}&=2^x(\log2)^2 \cdot \log 2 = 2^x(\log2)^3\\ \end{align*}

Every time we differentiate, we multiply the original function by another factor of \(\log 2\text{.}\) So, the \(n\)th derivative is given by:

\begin{align*} \ddiff{n}{}{x}\{2^x\}&=2^x(\log2)^n \end{align*}
