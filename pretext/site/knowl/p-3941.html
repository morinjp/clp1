EquationÂ 3.4.33 tells us that, when \(T_n(x)\) is the \(n\)th degree Taylor polynomial for a function \(f(x)\) about \(x=a\text{,}\) then

\begin{align*} |f(x)-T_n(x)| &= \left|\frac{f^{(n+1)}(c)}{(n+1)!}(x-a)^{n+1}\right|\\ \end{align*}

for some \(c\) strictly between \(x\) and \(a\text{.}\) In our case, \(n=3\text{,}\) \(a=0\text{,}\) \(x=2\text{,}\) and \(f^{(4)}(c)=e^c\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} |f(2)-T_3(2)| &= \left|\frac{f^{(4)}(c)}{4!}(2-0)^4\right|\\ &=\frac{2^4}{4!}e^c=\frac{2}{3}e^c\\ \end{align*}

Since \(c\) is strictly between 0 and 2, \(e^c \lt e^2\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} &\leq \frac{2}{3}e^2\\ \end{align*}

but this isn't a number we really know. Indeed: \(e^2\) is the very number we're trying to approximate. So, we use the estimation \(e \lt 3\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} & \lt \frac{2}{3}\cdot 3^2 =6 \end{align*}

We conclude that the error \(|f(2)-T_3(2)|\) is less than 6.
