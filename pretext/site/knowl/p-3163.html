The easiest way to figure out the area of the sector of an annulus (or a circle) is to figure out the area of the entire annulus, then multiply by what proportion of the entire annulus the sector is. For example, if your sector is \(\frac{1}{10}\) of the entire annulus, then its area is \(\frac{1}{10}\) of the area of the entire annulus. (See SectionÂ A.4 to see how this works out for circles.)
