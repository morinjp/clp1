\begin{align*} 7&=\frac{5x-9}{3x+7}\\ \end{align*}

and so

\begin{align*} 7(3x+7)&=5x-9\\ x&=-\frac{29}{8}\\ \end{align*}

So, when \(x=-\dfrac{29}{8}\text{,}\) our equation \(f(2x+1)=\dfrac{5x-9}{3x+7}\) becomes:

\begin{align*} f\left(2\cdot\frac{-29}{8}+1\right)&=\dfrac{5\cdot\frac{-29}{8}-9}{3\cdot\frac{-29}{8}+7}\\ \end{align*}

Or, equivalently:

\begin{align*} f\left(-\frac{25}{4}\right)&=7 \end{align*}
