\begin{align*} P(x)&=4x+2\sqrt{R^2-x^2}\\ \end{align*}

So, this is what we optimize. The endpoints of the domain for this function are \(x=0\) and \(x=R\text{.}\) To find the critical points, we differentiate:

\begin{align*} P'(x)&=4-\dfrac{2x}{\sqrt{R^2-x^2}}\\ P'(x)=0\iff 4&=\dfrac{2x}{\sqrt{R^2-x^2}}\\ x&=2\sqrt{R^2-x^2}\\ x^2&=4(R^2-x^2)\\ 5x^2&=4R^2\\ x&=\dfrac{2}{\sqrt{5}}R \end{align*}
