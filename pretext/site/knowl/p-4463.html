We will now consider a function \(f(x)\) that is defined on an interval \(I\text{,}\) except possibly at finitely many points of \(I\text{.}\) If \(f\) or its derivative \(f'\) is not defined at a point \(a\) of \(I\text{,}\) then we call \(a\) a singular point 1  of \(f\text{.}\)
