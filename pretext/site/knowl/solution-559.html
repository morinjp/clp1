Solution 3.4.11.7.1

We use the function \(f(x)=x^{1/3}\) and point \(a=27\) as the centre of our approximation since we can easily compute

\begin{align*} f(27)&=3 \end{align*}

We compute \(f'(x)=\dfrac{1}{3} x^{-2/3}\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} f'(27)&= \frac{1}{3} \cdot (27)^{-2/3} = \frac{1}{27} \end{align*}

So, the linear approximation of \(26^{1/3} = f(26)\) is

\begin{align*} 26^{1/3}&\approx T_1\left(26\right) = f(27) + f'(27)\cdot (26-27)\\ &= 3 - \frac{1}{27} = \frac{80}{27} \end{align*}
